Model Assumptions & Methodology

Automation Susceptibility
Each occupation's tasks are split into physical, routine-cognitive, and social buckets. Physical tasks are automatable proportional to dexterity × mobility capability. Routine cognitive proportional to reasoning (cap 90%). Social tasks hard-capped at 15%.

Robot Capability (S-Curve)
Logistic growth per dimension. Composite 2025=0.14 → 2030=0.49 → 2035=0.82 → 2040=0.96. Calibrated against Boston Dynamics / Figure / Tesla Optimus roadmaps.
Cost Curve
Base purchase price $150k in 2025, declining 12%/yr (Wright's Law, analogous to solar PV). Maintenance $8k/yr declining 5%/yr. Amortized over 7-year lifespan. Breakeven vs. median blue-collar worker: ~2028–2030.

Adoption Function
Industry-specific S-curve gated by ROI pressure, resistance, and regulatory lag. High-resistance sectors (healthcare, govt) lag 3–8 years. Scenarios: Slow (0.45×), Baseline (1.0×), Fast (1.65×) adoption multiplier.
Simulation based on BLS OOH structure · Quantitative assumptions explicitly modeled · Robot cost: Wright's Law · Adoption: ROI-gated logistic S-curve · Not a forecast — a structured scenario model.
